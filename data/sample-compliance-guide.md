## PHASE 1: INGREDIENT IMPORT FROM KOREA
## 1단계: 한국에서 원료 수입

### 1.1 Required Licence / 필요한 라이선스

**SFC Licence (Import Activity)**
- Legal basis: SFCA s.6(1), SFCR Part 4 s.47
- Apply through: My CFIA portal (https://inspection.canada.ca/en/food-licences/obtain-licence)
- Cost: $250 (valid 2 years)
- Processing time: 15-70 business days
- MUST be obtained BEFORE any import shipment
- Your SFC licence number must appear on EVERY import declaration (IID)
- Shipments will be REJECTED at the border without valid SFC licence

### 1.2 Preventive Control Plan for Imports / 수입용 예방관리계획 (PCP)

**Legal basis: SFCR Part 4 s.52-58**

Your import PCP must document:
1. **Korean supplier verification** - evidence that each Korean supplier meets Canadian food safety standards
2. **Hazard analysis per ingredient**:
   - Soy protein concentrate: allergen (soy), GMO risk, chemical contaminants
   - Gochujang: allergen (soy, wheat if contains wheat), food additives compliance
   - Red pepper powder: pesticide residues (MRL compliance), mycotoxins
   - Rice syrup: heavy metals, food additives
   - Soybean oil: GMO risk (if claiming Non-GMO), chemical contaminants
3. **Monitoring procedures** - how you verify each shipment
4. **Corrective actions** - what happens when non-compliance detected
5. **Traceability** - Korean supplier → Canadian distribution (2-year retention)

**CKFTA Benefits** (한-캐 FTA 혜택):
- Certificate of Origin required from each Korean exporter
- Use Canada Tariff Finder (https://www.tariffinder.ca/) for exact rates
- Many processed food ingredients have 0% tariff under CKFTA as of 2024

### 1.4 AIRS Requirements / AIRS 요건

**Check each ingredient in AIRS**: https://active.inspection.gc.ca/eng/imp/airse.asp
- Red pepper powder MAY require phytosanitary certificate from Korea's APQA
- Processed ingredients (soy protein, gochujang, rice syrup) generally do NOT require phyto certificates
- All food imports may be subject to CFIA inspection at point of entry

### 1.5 Import Documentation / 수입 서류

For each shipment, prepare:
- [ ] Commercial invoice
- [ ] Bill of lading / Air waybill
- [ ] Packing list
- [ ] Certificate of Origin (CKFTA format) for tariff preference
- [ ] Ingredient specifications / Certificate of Analysis (CoA)
- [ ] Phytosanitary certificate (if required by AIRS)
- [ ] Allergen declaration from supplier
- [ ] Non-GMO documentation from supplier (if claiming Non-GMO)

### 1.6 Food Additive Compliance / 식품 첨가물 적합성

**Legal basis: FDR B.01.301-B.01.305**

CRITICAL: Verify that EVERY additive in Korean ingredients is on Canada's Lists of Permitted Food Additives (Health Canada Marketing Authorizations). Some additives permitted in Korea are NOT permitted in Canada.

For each Korean ingredient, obtain a complete specification sheet listing ALL additives and verify against:
- Health Canada Lists of Permitted Food Additives
- URL: https://www.canada.ca/en/health-canada/services/food-nutrition/food-safety/food-additives.html

## PHASE 2: ALBERTA PROVINCIAL REQUIREMENTS
## 2단계: 알버타 주 요건

### 2.1 Food Handling Permit / 식품 취급 허가

**Legal basis: Alberta Food Regulation AR 31/2006, Public Health Act RSA 2000 c. P-37**

- Apply to: Alberta Health Services (AHS), Environmental Public Health
- URL: https://www.albertahealthservices.ca/eph/Page15563.aspx
- Apply at least **14 days before** opening
- Non-transferable permit
- Pre-opening inspection by Public Health Inspector
- Routine inspections every **4-6 months** after opening

### 2.2 Certified Food Handler / 공인 식품 취급자

**Legal basis: AR 31/2006 s.31**

If 6+ food handlers work simultaneously, at least one must hold:
- Alberta Food Safety Basics certification, OR
- Equivalent from CIFS, Acticert, etc.

### 2.3 Facility Requirements / 시설 요건

**Legal basis: Alberta Food Retail and Foodservices Code (2020)**

| Element | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Floors | Durable, smooth, non-absorbent, easily cleaned; floor drains in processing areas |
| Walls/Ceilings | Light-coloured, smooth, non-absorbent, durable |
| Lighting | Shatterproof covers on fixtures in food areas |
| Ventilation | Mechanical system required; stainless steel canopies/hoods |
| Water | Potable water ONLY for food contact |
| Equipment | Food-grade materials, easily cleanable |
| Hand washing | Dedicated stations with soap, hot/cold water, single-use towels |
| Pest control | Comprehensive pest management program |
| Waste | Adequate disposal systems |

## PHASE 3: FEDERAL PRODUCTION REQUIREMENTS
## 3단계: 연방 생산 요건

### 3.1 SFC Licence (Manufacturing Activity) / 제조 라이선스

**Legal basis: SFCA s.20, SFCR Part 4 s.47-51**

- Required for interprovincial trade or export
- Apply through My CFIA portal
- Cost: $250, valid 2 years
- Can combine with import activity on same licence

### 3.2 Production PCP / 생산 예방관리계획

**Legal basis: SFCR Part 4 s.52-58**

Your production PCP must address:

1. **Biological hazards**: Salmonella, Listeria, E. coli in processing environment
2. **Chemical hazards**:
   - Allergens (SOY, WHEAT - both are in your products)
   - Undeclared allergens from cross-contamination
   - Cleaning chemical residues
   - Soybean oil rancidity
3. **Physical hazards**: Foreign objects, equipment fragments
4. **Process controls**: Cooking temperatures for nuggets, pH monitoring for sauce
5. **Sanitation controls**: Cleaning schedule, verification
6. **Allergen controls**: See Phase 6 below
7. **Equipment maintenance**: Preventive maintenance schedule
8. **Employee hygiene**: Training, hand washing, illness policy
9. **Traceability**: Lot coding, ingredient tracing, finished product tracking

### 3.3 Traceability / 추적성

**Legal basis: SFCR Part 5 s.59-72**

- "One step back, one step forward" requirement
- Track: Korean supplier → your facility → your customers
- Documents must identify: food commodity, source, recipient, dates
- Retain records for minimum **2 years**
- Must provide to CFIA inspectors within **24 hours** on request

## PHASE 4: PRODUCT LABELLING
## 4단계: 제품 라벨링

### ⚠️ CRITICAL: SIMULATED POULTRY PRODUCT REQUIREMENTS

**Legal basis: CFIA Simulated Meat/Poultry Product Guidelines**
**URL: https://inspection.canada.ca/en/food-labels/labelling/industry/meat-and-poultry-products/simulated-products**

Your Vinker Crispy Chick'n nuggets are shaped to resemble chicken nuggets → **classified as a SIMULATED POULTRY PRODUCT**.

**MANDATORY REQUIREMENTS:**

1. **Product name MUST include "Simulated"**:
   - ✅ "Simulated Chicken Nuggets" / "Croquettes de poulet simulées"
   - ✅ "Plant-Based Simulated Chicken Nuggets"
   - ❌ "Crispy Chick'n" (NOT compliant)
   - ❌ "Plant-Based Chicken Nuggets" without "simulated" (NOT compliant)
   - ❌ "Chick'n Nuggets" (NOT compliant)

2. **"Contains no poultry" / "Ne contient pas de volaille"**:
   - Must appear on principal display panel
   - In close proximity to common name
   - Same size and prominence as common name

3. **Protein requirements**:
   - Must meet minimum protein content equivalent to real chicken nuggets
   - Protein Rating must be ≥20 per reasonable daily intake
   - Soy protein concentrate has acceptable PER

4. **Fortification with vitamins/minerals**:
   - Must match nutritional profile of real chicken nuggets
   - Add: Thiamine, Riboflavin, Niacin, B6, Folic Acid, B12, Pantothenic Acid, Iron, Zinc
   - Declare all additions on Nutrition Facts table

### 4.2 Nutrition Facts Table / 영양성분표

**Legal basis: FDR B.01.401-B.01.603**

- MUST use **Canadian format** (different from Korean/US format)
- Serving size must follow Canadian Reference Amounts (SFCR Schedule M)
- For simulated chicken nuggets: include added vitamins/minerals
- Core nutrients: Calories, Fat (sat/trans), Cholesterol, Sodium, Carbohydrate (fibre/sugars), Protein, Vit D, Calcium, Iron, Potassium
- Additional for simulated products: all fortified vitamins/minerals with absolute amounts and %DV

## PHASE 5: CERTIFICATIONS
## 5단계: 인증

### 5.1 Non-GMO Certification / Non-GMO 인증

**Status: VOLUNTARY (자발적)**

**Legal basis: CAN/CGSB-32.315, FDA s.5(1)**

YOUR HIGH-RISK INGREDIENTS for GMO:
- Soy protein concentrate → HIGH RISK (90%+ of N. American soy is GMO)
- Soybean oil → HIGH RISK
- Cornstarch → HIGH RISK (corn is widely GMO)
- Cane sugar → LOW RISK (sugar cane is not commercially GMO)

**Korean-sourced soy**: Korea primarily uses non-GMO soy domestically, but DOCUMENTATION IS REQUIRED:
- Certificate of Analysis (CoA) showing non-GMO status
- IP (Identity Preserved) documentation from Korean supplier
- PCR testing results (for Non-GMO Project Verified)

**Recommended approach:**
1. Obtain Non-GMO Project Verified certification (most recognized)
2. Apply at: https://www.nongmoproject.org/get-non-gmo-verified/
3. Select a Technical Administrator from approved list
4. Prepare: supplier documentation, SOPs, CoAs for all ingredients
5. Testing WILL be required for soy and corn ingredients
6. Timeline: 3-6 months
7. Cost: varies by TA and product count

**Label rules for Non-GMO claims:**
- ✅ "Made with Non-GMO Soybean Oil" (specific claim)
- ✅ Non-GMO Project Verified seal (after certification)
- ❌ "100% Non-GMO" (absolute terms prohibited)
- ❌ "GMO-Free" (absolute terms prohibited)
- ❌ "Non-GMO Rice Syrup" (no commercial GMO rice exists = misleading)

### 5.2 Organic Certification / 유기 인증

**Status: VOLUNTARY but REGULATED (자발적이나 규제됨)**

**Legal basis: SFCR Part 13, CAN/CGSB-32.310, CAN/CGSB-32.311**

**Assessment for Vinker products:**
- Most ingredients are NOT currently organic
- To use "Organic" on the product: 95%+ of ingredients must be certified organic
- If some ingredients are organic: can list "organic [ingredient]" in ingredient list (no certification needed for <70%)

**Recommendation: DEFER organic certification** unless you source certified organic ingredients. If pursuing later:
1. Source certified organic ingredients (Korea has equivalency with Canada)
2. Get your Alberta facility certified organic by a CFIA-accredited CB
3. Timeline: 3-4 months for processor certification
4. Cost: $300-$2,000+ initial, plus annual renewal

### 5.3 Vegan Certification / 비건 인증

**Status: VOLUNTARY (자발적)**

**Assessment for Vinker products:**
- Both products appear to contain NO animal-derived ingredients ✅
- Plant-based protein (soy) ✅
- No dairy, eggs, honey, gelatin, etc. ✅

**Recommended approach:**
1. Obtain certification from one of:
   - **BeVeg International** (ISO 17065 accredited) - recommended for credibility
   - **Vegan Action** (widely recognized in North America)
   - **The Vegan Society** (globally recognized)
2. Process: application, ingredient review, facility audit
3. Timeline: 1-3 months
4. Cost: varies ($500-$2,000+ depending on body)

**Label rules:**
- ✅ "Vegan" with certification logo
- ✅ "Plant-Based" (no certification needed but must be truthful)
- Both must be substantiable if challenged by CFIA

## PHASE 6: ALLERGEN MANAGEMENT
## 6단계: 알레르기 유발 물질 관리

### 6.1 Your Priority Allergens / 우선 알레르기 유발 물질

**Legal basis: FDR B.01.010.1-B.01.010.4**

| Allergen | Present In | Status |
|---|---|---|
| **SOY** | Nuggets (soy protein, soybean oil, soybeans), Sauce (fermented soybean powder) | DECLARED ✅ |
| **WHEAT** | Nuggets (wheat flour, wheat in breading) | DECLARED ✅ |
| Wheat in Sauce? | CHECK: Does the malt in gochujang contain wheat? If barley malt → gluten source | VERIFY ⚠️ |

### 6.2 Allergen Control Plan (part of PCP) / 알레르기 유발 물질 관리 계획

**Legal basis: SFCR Part 4 Div 2-5, CFIA Allergen Preventive Controls**

Since BOTH your products contain soy:
- Cross-contamination between products is less critical for soy
- But: if your facility also produces soy-free or wheat-free products in the future, full allergen segregation will be required

Current requirements:
1. **Receiving**: Verify allergen status of all incoming ingredients
2. **Storage**: Clearly label all allergen-containing ingredients
3. **Production**: Document allergen presence at each production step
4. **Labelling**: Verify correct labels on correct products (label control procedure)
5. **Training**: All staff trained on allergen awareness
6. **Cleaning**: Validated cleaning between different product runs

### 6.3 Malt/Wheat in Gochujang - CRITICAL CHECK / 고추장 내 맥아/밀 - 중요 확인

**ACTION REQUIRED**: Verify with your Korean gochujang supplier:
- What is the source of "Malt" in the gochujang recipe?
- If barley malt → contains GLUTEN → must declare "Contains: Soy, Wheat (gluten)" or "Contains: Soy, Barley (gluten)"
- If wheat-derived malt → must declare Wheat
- This affects your Gochujang Sauce allergen declaration

## CRITICAL ISSUES & WARNINGS
## 중요 이슈 및 경고

### ⚠️ ISSUE 1: Product Naming (HIGHEST PRIORITY)

**"Vinker Crispy Chick'n" is NOT compliant as a product name in Canada.**

Under CFIA simulated product rules, since the product resembles chicken nuggets, the common name MUST include "simulated." Options:
- "Vinker Crispy Chick'n - Simulated Chicken Nuggets"
- "Vinker - Plant-Based Simulated Chicken Nuggets"

The brand name "Vinker Crispy Chick'n" can appear as a brand/trade name, but the **regulatory common name** must include "simulated" and "Contains no poultry" must be prominently displayed.

### ⚠️ ISSUE 2: Fortification Requirements

Simulated chicken nuggets MUST be fortified with B vitamins, Iron, and Zinc to match real chicken nuggets. This requires:
- Sourcing food-grade vitamin/mineral premix
- Precise addition during manufacturing
- Lab testing to verify levels
- Declaration on Nutrition Facts table

### ⚠️ ISSUE 3: Gochujang Sub-Ingredients

The allergen declaration for Gochujang Sauce depends on:
- Whether "Malt" is from barley or wheat (affects gluten/wheat declaration)
- Whether any processing aids contain allergens
- Get a COMPLETE specification from Korean supplier

### ⚠️ ISSUE 4: Canadian Nutrition Facts Format

The Nutrition Facts table must be in the SPECIFIC Canadian format, not Korean or US format. Key differences:
- Canadian uses metric serving sizes per Schedule M reference amounts
- Different nutrient order than Korea/US
- % Daily Value based on Canadian Daily Values (different from Korean DRI)
- Must be bilingual (English/French)

### ⚠️ ISSUE 5: Non-GMO Soybean Oil Labelling

Your product lists "NON-GMO Soybean Oil." Making this claim on the Canadian label:
- Must be substantiable with documentation
- Korean soy is generally non-GMO, but need CoA/IP documentation
- Consider formal Non-GMO Project Verified certification for credibility
- Cannot use absolute terms ("100% Non-GMO" or "GMO-Free")
